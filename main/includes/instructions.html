Overview

In this experiment you will use a movie recommendation system similar to systems used on online platforms. The system learns from ratings to improve predictions.

You start with an endowment of EUR 2.50. You can keep this amount or spend part of it to rate movies. Your final payment depends on your choices, other participants' choices, and how well the system guesses your private movie ranking.

The Experiment Has Three Parts

  1. Part 1: A short survey and a ranking of 5 movies from most preferred to least preferred. This ranking is your private "true preference" and is not shown to other participants.
  2. Part 2: {{ num_rounds }} interaction rounds. In each round, you choose whether to rate or skip a movie. Rating costs EUR 0.25. Skipping has no cost.
  3. Part 3: Final payment based on your rating decisions and how well the system guesses your Part 1 ranking.

Part 1: Survey and Your Private Movie Ranking (alone)

  • You answer a short survey about opinions.
  • You rank 5 movies by dragging items into your preferred order.
  • This ranking is private and is used later for evaluating prediction quality. The closer the final guess is to your ranking, the higher your bonus.

Part 2: Interaction Rounds

You play {{ num_rounds }} rounds. You start these rounds with EUR 2.50. Each time you choose Rate, you pay EUR 0.25. Skipping has no cost. Remaining budget is added to your final payment.

Every rating (yours or your partner's) helps the recommendation system learn. If both rate in a round, learning improves more than if only one rates.

One-round outcomes

Partner: skip (no cost) Partner: rate (pay EUR 0.25)
You: skip (no cost) No improvement System learns a bit
You: rate (pay EUR 0.25) System learns a bit System learns more

Approximate learning rule: each rating increases expected accuracy by about 0.10 correctly guessed movie positions on average.

Part 3: Final Bonus and Payment

Payment formula:
Final Payment = (EUR 2.50 - EUR 0.25 x your ratings) + (EUR 2 x number of correct guesses)
